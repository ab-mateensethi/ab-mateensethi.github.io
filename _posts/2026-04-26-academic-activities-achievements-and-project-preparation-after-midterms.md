After the midterm exams, our regular classes and labs started again according to the university schedule. Most teachers were advising students to improve themselves because many students did not perform well in the mids. My overall performance was good, although I faced some difficulty in a few papers, and I was waiting for the results. During the first weekend after mids, I went to a swimming pool in Faisalabad with my friends because the weather was very hot. I enjoy exploring new places, so it was a good experience for me. Along with enjoying the outing, I also completed my pending lab tasks such as OOP and Database assignments and submitted them online. I continued studying all subjects side by side because I believe regular study is the best way to manage academic pressure and prepare for exams effectively.

The following week, classes continued normally, and we finally received our checked Calculus-II midterm and quiz. Alhamdulillah, I achieved the highest marks in the entire Computer Engineering section with 30/30 in the midterm and 10/10 in the quiz. My teacher even called my paper the "Best Paper Ever," which became a very memorable moment for me because Mathematics has always been my favorite subject. Previously, I had also scored 90/100 with an A grade in Calculus-I during the first semester. At the same time, our semester projects were also starting. Our DLD teacher, Sir Abdullah Bilal, instructed us to prepare an IC-based project without using controllers. After discussing ideas with seniors and researching on the internet, I selected the project "Digital ATM PIN Verification System" because it was unique and based on a real-world application. My group members and I prepared a complete proposal, including the working mechanism, required components and project model, and our teacher appreciated and approved the idea.

As the semester continued, our OOP professor, Dr. Akram, taught us interesting topics like Inheritance and Polymorphism in great detail, and I practiced coding daily to improve my concepts. We also had an OOP quiz during the same week, and although students faced some time management issues, we managed it successfully. Meanwhile, our Database teacher also gave instructions regarding the database project, and our OOP lab mini project was about to be assigned. Since project work was increasing, I went to the Faisalabad market with one of my day scholar friends to buy the required components for our DLD project. We purchased breadboards, logic ICs, resistors, bulbs and other electronic items needed for the project. Along with project shopping, I also bought some personal items and then returned to the hostel.
