After the Eid holidays and online classes ended, the government announced the resumption of regular classes. Due to economic concerns and an oil crisis, Friday was declared an off day, but all schools, colleges and universities were instructed to restart physical classes. I was very happy with this decision because I faced many difficulties during online learning. Sometimes there were internet issues, sometimes the environment was not suitable for studying and occasionally even teachers faced connectivity problems. Despite these challenges, I maintained my consistency, worked hard and submitted all assignments on time. This period taught me the importance of discipline and self-motivation.

Regular classes officially resumed on April 1, 2026 and I prepared to return to university. After packing my belongings and taking prayers and blessings from my parents, I started my journey. Usually, I travel by train and reach my university by around 1:30 PM, but that day the train stopped due to a technical fault and remained halted for about two and a half hours. It was a stressful moment for me because I was traveling alone and I had a Database lecture at 2:00 PM which I really wanted to attend. Unfortunately, I missed that lecture. However, one of my friends came to pick me up from the station as I had a lot of luggage and he helped me understand what was covered in class that day.

The next day, regular classes continued and I had a Digital Logic Design quiz. I had prepared well through practice and Alhamdulillah, the quiz went well. Soon after, our midterm examinations started. Compared to the first semester, I experienced that the second semester was more challenging. Online classes had created some pending work, so I had to work harder day and night to prepare. Although there were a few mistakes, I attempted all my papers confidently. We had six subjects in total, excluding labs and after putting in consistent effort, the midterms finally concluded on April 15, 2026. On the same day, all students received their official student ID cards and university Google IDs, which allowed us to access premium academic tools and improve our learning experience.

After completing the exams, we requested our teachers to grant us one extra day off so that students coming from distant cities could visit home and refresh themselves. As I travel nearly five and a half hours, the additional time was helpful. I spent quality time with my family, met old school and college friends and discussed my academic journey, projects and future goals. During these holidays, our Digital Logic Design professor asked us to prepare lab projects based on previous experiments. I discussed ideas with my group members, consulted seniors and explored AI tools to shortlist topics. We decided to finalize the proposal after meeting our instructor. After four days of refreshing holidays, I returned to university on April 19, 2026, with renewed energy, motivation and the prayers of my parents, ready to continue my academic journey with dedication.
