The fourth week of the semester became very important because our Database professor Dr. Bilal, officially announced the semester project. In this project, we had to continue the work related to Python programming and Machine Learning that we had studied in the first semester. Sir instructed us to select authentic real-world datasets from Kaggle with proper sources, licenses and large amounts of real data instead of artificially generated datasets. The main objective of this semester project was not only to train machine learning models but also to integrate and retrieve the data through databases using 1NF, 2NF and 3NF normalization techniques and finally deploy the trained model properly. This was a completely new skill for us, so I was very excited to learn it. Our groups consisted of two students and initially my friend and I selected datasets like Heart Disease Prediction and others, but unfortunately some of them were rejected because they were not fully authentic according to the project requirements. After detailed research on Kaggle, we finally selected three strong real-world datasets: Credit Card Transaction Fraud Detection, Heart Failure Prediction and Air Quality Prediction. We carefully reviewed and prepared all three datasets because the following week we had to present them again for approval.

At the same time, our OOP lab instructor Mam Rimsha Chaudhary, assigned us a very interesting and skill-improving mini project called "University Admission Process System." In this project, we had to design a complete console-based university management system similar to a student LMS for course registration, fee management and academic records by using a 3-Tier Architecture. Along with that, we had to implement all major Object-Oriented Programming concepts such as Abstraction, Encapsulation, Inheritance and Polymorphism using C#. This was our semester mini project before the final major project. We were given a deadline of three weeks and I started working on it step by step by first understanding the requirements and then implementing each feature carefully. As a result of continuous practice and strong concepts, I completed my project one week before the deadline. I also attached the complete screenshots and results of my project work.

Alongside technical subjects, our Communication Skills teacher also continued assigning activities to improve our confidence and speaking abilities throughout the semester. Previously, we had participated in tasks like debates and this time Mam assigned us a five-minute English vlog project. The requirements were that the video had to be recorded without pauses, we had to be properly dressed and we could discuss any topic, interact with people or share our thoughts in English. During the weekend, I went outside my department with my friends and one of my friends recorded my vlog in the department garden area. In the video, I introduced my department, talked about the beauty and benefits of the garden, explained how students spend time there and also discussed how I utilize my weekends and holidays. I even interacted with one of my friends and included his opinions in the vlog. Overall, it was a very enjoyable and confidence-building assignment for me and I completed it successfully while preparing myself for future activities and presentations.
